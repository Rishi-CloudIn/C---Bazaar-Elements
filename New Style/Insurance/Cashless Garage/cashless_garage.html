What is a network garage?

Most car insurers today provide you the facility to get your car repaired at authorised garages, which are in the network of the insurance company. Network garages are identified by insurers based on their ability to provide:

  • Fair and honest service
  • Transparency in pricing
  • Services or repairs as per the agreement with the insurer
  • Service costs that are in alignment with the initial quotes
  • Well-trained staff who are keen on doing the repair work efficiently
  • A quick and easy procedure for raising complaints
Cashless claims at network garages - How it works

The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 mandates that all vehicles plying on Indian roads should be protected with at least a minimum insurance, i.e., the third-party liability insurance. This owner from losses due to death, injury, or property damage to third-party. Apart from the standard cover for motor vehicles, insurance providers also offer cashless facilities for the settlement of claims. Under the cashless insurance cover, the car owner can choose to get his vehicle repaired at a service station that has a tie-up with the insurer. In this scenario, the customer is not expected to pay for the repair work upfront; once the repairs are done, the insurance provider will settle the expenses with the garage.

Most insurers have thousands of network garages spread across different locations in the country. If you have opted for cashless insurance, you will just have to take the vehicle to an authorised garage from the pre-defined list specified in your policy.

The process of cashless claims follow the path specified below:
  1. When the insured car is damaged in an accident, it is taken to a network garage for repair.
  2. An inspection of the car is performed, followed by the issuance of an approval for cashless claim settlement.
  3. The service centre then performs the repair work on the vehicle and provides the bills and other relevant documents to the insurer. The evaluation of bills and documents are performed by the insurer in a thorough manner.
  4. After all the necessary evaluation, verification, and investigation, the bill amount is paid by the insurance company to the garage.
  5. The customer is required to bear the compulsory deductible, voluntary deductible, and the depreciated component of the claim.
  6. In case it has been found that the car has been damaged beyond economical repair, the insurance company will offer to pay the customer the existing market value of the vehicle. This value is calculated based on a few factors, such as the car's mileage, general condition, and age. You can request for this amount to be reconsidered if you think your vehicle is worth more than the amount offered.
